# The Constitution Against the Climate

## Opening — The Document Under the Fifth-Pass Architecture

This document is the Part IX paradigmatic-stress-test document of the fifth-pass constitutional project. Under the fifth-pass framing, climate operates as paradigmatic stress test of the existing constitutional architecture within the broader Part II disturbance-regimes architecture (Documents B, S, T, U, V, W, C, 4) rather than as freestanding climate-constitutional analysis. The substantive engagement preserves the existing project record's substantive analysis — six structural constitutional failures (Part One); nine ecological-governance amendments B.1 through B.9 (Part Two); multigenerational implementation, currency architecture, and the cooperative imperative (Part Three); integration and forward implications (Part Four) — and re-specifies the document at four load-bearing points.

The project rests on a load-bearing triad: biocultural governance as constitutional ontology, eco-mimetic policy-making as operational method, disturbance-adapted implementation as temporal orientation. The Indigenous and Eastern traditions are primary articulators; the Western complex-adaptive-systems, ecology, biomimicry, and disturbance-ecology canons are recent and partial recovery.

The four load-bearing re-specifications: First, the six structural constitutional failures are re-framed as scale-mismatch failures — decision rights operating at the wrong scale of the nested-scales reference frame Document K (Part V) articulates. Second, the nine proposed amendments are re-framed as bounded-decision-right specifications with scope, scale, feedback condition, and sunset-or-adaptation condition per provision — coordinated with the Part XII Documents D-1, D-2, D-3, D-4 structural-commitments architecture. Third, the three implementation threads (multigenerational institutional design; dual currency architecture; competitive vs. cooperative framework) are re-framed as cultivation conditions — coordinated with Documents H (Part XII intergenerational rectification), E (Part XII currency architecture), and the broader cultivation-protocol Document D-Synthesis (Part XIII). Fourth, the document integrates the CAS-and-disturbance-ecology canon Document K specifies and the material-substrate documents N, O, P, Q (Part VI), and reads climate under the broader Part II disturbance-regimes architecture.

## Part One — Six Structural Constitutional Failures Re-Framed as Scale-Mismatch Failures

The substantive analysis of the six structural constitutional failures the existing project record carries is preserved through re-framing under the nested-scales reference frame. Each failure is read as substantive scale-mismatch failure of decision rights operating at the wrong scale of the nested-scales reference frame. The substantive failures include decision-right scope exceeding decision-right scale (sub-scale decisions absorbing super-scale impacts: state-level fossil-fuel decisions producing biosphere-scale outcomes); temporal scale-mismatch between decision-right horizon and ecological-system temporal scale (electoral-cycle-time decisions producing biosphere-scale legacy impacts across geological timescales); the substantive cumulative-impact architecture failure (single-jurisdiction decisions on substantive cumulative-impact substrate that the substantive decision-rights architecture cannot register at appropriate scale); the substantive federalism scale-mismatch (state-level versus federal-level versus international-level decision-rights conflicts under substantive climate conditions); the substantive separation-of-powers failure under substantive climate-disturbance conditions (executive-legislative-judicial scale-mismatch under substantive emergency-and-multi-temporal conditions); the substantive cumulative-historical-impact failure (decisions made over substantial historical periods producing substantive contemporary disturbance-regime conditions the substantive contemporary decision-rights architecture cannot adequately address). The substantive coordination operates with Document K (Part V) on the substantive nested-scales reference frame articulation.

## Part Two — Nine Amendments Re-Framed as Bounded-Decision-Right Specifications

The substantive analysis of the nine proposed amendments B.1 through B.9 is preserved through re-framing under the bounded-decision-right specification architecture Documents D-1 through D-4 specify. The substantive re-specification coordinates with Documents D-1 through D-4. Amendments B.1–B.3 coordinate with D-1 (foundational decision rights at biosphere-and-ecozone scales) on substantive planetary-boundary-honoring constitutional commitments. The public-trust-and-accountability amendments coordinate with D-2 (public-check decision rights at ecosystem-and-habitat scales). The substantive resource-provisioning amendments coordinate with D-3 (provisioning decision rights at habitat-and-population scales). The substantive temporal-intergenerational amendments coordinate with D-4 (cognitive and intergenerational decision rights).

The substantive analytical refusal is of the engineered-fixture framing of amendment-as-finalized-text; amendments under the cultivation framing are substantive bounded-decision-right specifications whose substantive operational instantiation is substantive cultivation work rather than substantive fixed-text engineered fixture.
