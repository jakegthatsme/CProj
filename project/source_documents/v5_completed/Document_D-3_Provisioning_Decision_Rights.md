# Provisioning Decision Rights

## Opening — The Document Under the Fifth-Pass Architecture

This document is the Part XII structural-commitments document specifying provisioning decision rights at habitat-and-population scales. The project rests on a load-bearing triad: biocultural governance as constitutional ontology, eco-mimetic policy-making as operational method, disturbance-adapted implementation as temporal orientation. The Indigenous and Eastern traditions are primary articulators; the Western complex-adaptive-systems, ecology, biomimicry, and disturbance-ecology canons are recent and partial recovery.

Document D-3 specifies substantive provisioning decision rights at habitat-and-population scales: the publicly funded research returns and the substantive material-substrate decision rights (the cultivation-condition specifications Documents N, O, P, Q of Part VI derive) as bounded decision rights under the four-element specification format.

**Scope:** The right operates over substantive disposition of outputs (data, intellectual property, methodology, publications, materials) from publicly funded research; the right specifies substantively that outputs return to substantive public substrate rather than to substantive private appropriation.

**Scale:** Habitat-and-population, where substantive public-research funding operates and where the substantive research-output disposition affects substantive community-and-locality cultivation.

**Feedback condition:** The substantive disposition-of-outputs regime operates through substantive specified disclosure, license, and public-access architecture. The substantive Bayh-Dole Act regime (35 U.S.C. §§ 200–212) is engaged critically through substantive Robin Feldman and Janet Freilich's substantive scholarship on substantive Bayh-Dole critique. The substantive public-domain commitments through the substantive contemporary open-science movement (Michael Nielsen's *Reinventing Discovery: The New Era of Networked Science*, 2011); the substantive commons-based-peer-production tradition through Yochai Benkler's *The Wealth of Networks: How Social Production Transforms Markets and Freedom* (2006) provides substantive substrate.

**Sunset-or-adaptation condition:** The substantive disposition regime is subject to substantive scheduled review under substantive specified adaptive-cycle phase.
