# Multi-Eyed Seeing as Constitutional Method: Methodology, Epistemology, and the Architecture of Plural Authority

## Opening — The Document Under the Fifth-Pass Architecture

This document is the methodological foundation of the fifth-pass constitutional project — Part VII anchor on multi-eyed seeing as constitutional method. The project rests on a load-bearing triad: biocultural governance as constitutional ontology, eco-mimetic policy-making as operational method, disturbance-adapted implementation as temporal orientation. The Indigenous and Eastern traditions are the principle's primary articulators; the Western complex-adaptive-systems, ecology, biomimicry, and disturbance-ecology canons are recent and partial recovery.

Under the fifth-pass framing, the document is preserved with targeted edits. The substantive analytical work of the existing project record on multi-eyed seeing as constitutional method — the foundational engagement with the Marshall family's Two-Eyed Seeing (Etuaptmumk) articulation, the Indigenous research-methodology tradition, the standpoint-epistemology canon, the epistemic-injustice literature, the decolonial-theory canon, the neurodivergent-epistemic-contribution literature, the application to the project's architecture, and the renovation-versus-decolonial engagement — is preserved through targeted edits. The fifth-pass edits operate at three load-bearing points. First, the Eastern epistemic traditions are integrated at primary-articulator constitutional standing alongside the Indigenous-articulator standing the existing project record establishes through the Marshalls. Second, the renovation-versus-decolonial question is re-engaged under the cultivation-rather-than-design-renovation framing — the document's most consequential targeted edit, the analytical move on which the project's substantive answer to the decolonial critique turns. Third, the cultivation framing is propagated throughout the existing analytical Parts as integrative re-reading rather than as rewriting.

The Marshall family's Two-Eyed Seeing (Etuaptmumk) framework — articulated by Mi'kmaw Elders Albert Marshall and Murdena Marshall together with Cheryl Bartlett in their 2012 essay "Two-Eyed Seeing and Other Lessons Learned within a Co-Learning Journey of Bringing Together Indigenous and Mainstream Knowledges and Ways of Knowing" in the *Journal of Environmental Studies and Sciences* — remains the methodological core. Etuaptmumk, the Mi'kmaw word the Marshalls translate as "the gift of multiple perspectives," names the discipline of seeing with one eye through the strengths of Indigenous ways of knowing and with the other eye through the strengths of Western ways of knowing, and using both together. The multi-eyed extension honors rather than displaces the bilateral framework; the extension follows the Marshalls' instruction outward into the multi-traditional canonical commitment the project carries.

The self-reflexive question the existing project record carries — whether the multilateral extension domesticates underrepresented traditions or permits genuine co-constitution — is sharpened rather than dissolved under the cultivation framing. Cultivation does not domesticate because cultivation does not synthesize; the conditions under which traditions continue to grow alongside one another in their distinctive registers are what the methodological discipline maintains, not a single architectural register the discipline produces. ## Part One — From Two-Eyed Seeing to Multi-Eyed Seeing: The Conceptual Development

The substantive Mi'kmaw articulation grounds the commitment in *Etuaptmumk* — the gift of multiple perspectives — as foundational Indigenous methodological commitment rather than as comparative supplement to Western methodology.

The substantive priority of the Marshalls' articulation is essential to the project's continuing commitment. The two-eyed seeing framework is Mi'kmaw articulation engaged with Western scientific traditions on Mi'kmaw analytical terms; it is not Western multidisciplinarity extended to include Indigenous knowledge as one among several inputs. The methodological commitment under the Marshalls' articulation is that Indigenous ways of knowing carry substantive epistemic standing co-equal with Western ways of knowing, with the substantive engagement maintaining the integrity of each tradition rather than absorbing one into the other.

The multi-eyed extension the project carries — the simultaneous engagement of multiple analytical traditions whose authority the project recognizes as co-equal — is an extension of the Marshalls' framework rather than a displacement. The extension follows the bilateral methodological commitment outward into the multi-traditional canonical inheritance the project commits to: Western constitutional-democratic; Indigenous constitutional in its many nations; Black-radical; dalit and South Asian anti-caste; disability-justice; neurodivergent; feminist and Black-feminist; Latinx and Chicanx; Asian-American; decolonial-theory; climate-justice; African; Islamic; Latin American and Andean; Caribbean; Pacific Islander; Jewish. Each tradition stands at primary-articulator standing in its own analytical register.

The self-reflexive engagement the existing project record articulates — whether the multilateral extension domesticates the underrepresented traditions or permits genuine co-constitution — is itself part of the analytical work the methodological commitment carries. The cultivation framing's analytical move is to specify that cultivation, unlike synthesis-into-architecture, does not domesticate; the conditions under which the traditions continue to grow alongside one another in their distinctive registers are what the methodological commitment maintains. Under the fifth-pass cultivation framing, the Indigenous research-methodology tradition is re-read as primary articulation of cultivation-condition methodology — the conditions under which research that engages Indigenous peoples and the more-than-human world is to be conducted under Indigenous epistemic-and-ethical commitments rather than under Western methodological frameworks evaluated against Indigenous knowledge. The substantive analytical engagement preserves the Indigenous research-methodology tradition's articulations in their substantive registers; the cultivation framing positions these articulations as primary articulators of methodological cultivation conditions that the Western qualitative-research literature partially recovers as recent-and-partial recovery rather than as foundational articulation.

The Indigenous research-methodology tradition's substantive commitments — relational accountability; ceremony as substantive constitutive engagement; story and oral tradition as substantive epistemic engagement; community-based research conducted by and with Indigenous communities; substantive engagement with Indigenous languages as substantive epistemic-ontological commitment; substantive engagement with land as substantive constitutive relation rather than as research site — are foundational cultivation-condition methodological specifications that the project's broader methodological discipline operates under.

## Part Seven — Eastern Epistemic Traditions at Primary-Articulator Standing

The Eastern epistemic traditions stand at primary-articulator constitutional-methodological standing alongside the Indigenous-articulator standing the existing project record establishes through the Marshall family. ### The Confucian Epistemic Tradition

The Confucian epistemic tradition runs through the *Analects*' commitments on *zhi* (knowing) and the rectification of names — engaged through Bryan Van Norden's translation and Edward Slingerland's *Confucius: Analects, with Selections from Traditional Commentaries* (2003); through the *Doctrine of the Mean* (*Zhongyong*) on *cheng* (sincerity) as substantive epistemic-moral commitment, engaged through Roger Ames and David Hall's *Focusing the Familiar: A Translation and Philosophical Interpretation of the Zhongyong* (2001); through Wang Yangming's *Instructions for Practical Living* (the *Chuanxilu*) on *liangzhi* (innate moral knowledge) and the unity of knowledge and action (*zhi xing he yi*); through Zhu Xi's substantive engagement with *gewu* (investigation of things) and the Cheng brothers' substantive engagement with *li* (pattern/principle) and *qi* (vital energy/material force) as foundational epistemic-ontological architecture; through the contemporary engagement through Tu Weiming's *Confucian Thought: Selfhood as Creative Transformation* (1985), Wm. Theodore de Bary's *Asian Values and Human Rights* (1998), Roger Ames and David Hall's *Thinking Through Confucius* (1987) and *Thinking from the Han: Self, Truth, and Transcendence in Chinese and Western Culture* (1998).

The substantive Confucian epistemic commitment integrates knowing-and-action as substantively unified — Wang Yangming's *zhi xing he yi* commitment that knowledge that does not issue in action is not substantively knowledge — and integrates moral cultivation as substantively constitutive of epistemic adequacy. The substantive contemporary engagement with the Confucian epistemic tradition through the contemporary scholarship provides foundational primary-articulator substrate that the Western pragmatist epistemic tradition through Peirce, James, and Dewey partially recovers as recent-and-partial recovery.

### The Daoist Epistemic Tradition

The Daoist epistemic tradition runs through the *Daodejing* and the *Zhuangzi* on *wu wei* knowing and the perspectival epistemology articulated in the *Zhuangzi*'s "Equality of Things" chapter (*Qiwulun*). The substantive engagement runs through Brook Ziporyn's *Zhuangzi: The Essential Writings, with Selections from Traditional Commentaries* (2009) and Philip J. Ivanhoe's *The Daodejing of Laozi* (2002). The Daoist commitment to perspectival epistemology — the substantive analytical claim that knowing operates from substantive perspectives rather than from a putative view-from-nowhere — anticipates by more than two millennia the substantive engagement with situated and standpoint epistemology that Western feminist epistemology articulates as substantive recent commitment. The Daoist critique of fixed-concept thinking and the substantive commitment to the contingent character of conceptual frameworks provides foundational primary-articulator substrate that the Western late-twentieth-century epistemic engagement with conceptual scheme dependence partially recovers.

### The Buddhist Madhyamaka and Yogacara Traditions

The Buddhist Madhyamaka tradition founded by Nagarjuna (circa 150–250 CE) articulates substantive engagement with the two-truths doctrine (conventional truth and ultimate truth) and the analysis of emptiness (*śūnyatā*) as foundational epistemic-ontological commitment. The substantive engagement runs through Jay L. Garfield's *The Fundamental Wisdom of the Middle Way: Nāgārjuna's Mūlamadhyamakakārikā* (1995) and *Engaging Buddhism: Why It Matters to Philosophy* (2015). The two-truths doctrine articulates the substantive analytical claim that engagement with the world operates through the conventional categorizations that organize ordinary cognition while remaining aware that those categorizations are themselves constituted relationally and lack independent self-existence. The substantive methodological commitment is foundational engagement with the constituted character of analytical categories — the substantive analytical commitment that the categories through which engagement operates are themselves substantively under analytical engagement rather than fixed-in-advance.

The Yogacara tradition founded by Asanga (circa 300–370 CE) and Vasubandhu (circa 316–396 CE) extends the substantive engagement into the analytical psychology of cognition — the eight-consciousness doctrine, the *alaya-vijnana* (storehouse consciousness), and the analysis of how the world appears to cognition. The substantive engagement through Garfield (2015) and the broader contemporary scholarship provides foundational primary-articulator substrate for substantive engagement with cognitive substrate that the contemporary Western cognitive-science tradition partially recovers.

The Chan/Zen epistemic discipline through Huineng's *Platform Sutra* (Plat-tsung-ching, *Liuzu tan jing*) and Dogen's *Shobogenzo* (in particular the *Genjokoan*) articulates substantive engagement with non-dual cognition and the substantive practice of sustained attention as substantive epistemic discipline. The substantive contemporary engagement extends through the broader scholarship.

### The Classical Indian Darshana Traditions

The classical Indian *darshana* schools — the six philosophical schools of Indian philosophy (Nyaya, Vaisheshika, Samkhya, Yoga, Mimamsa, Vedanta) and their distinctive *pramana* (epistemic-authority) theories — provide foundational substantive engagement at primary-articulator standing. ## Part Eight — The Renovation-Versus-Decolonial Question Re-Engaged Under Cultivation Framing

The renovation-versus-decolonial question is the most consequential analytical engagement the document's fifth-pass re-framing operationalizes. The substantive decolonial critique — that the project's analytical move from Western frame to multi-traditional canonical commitment risks being itself a Western synthesis that absorbs the traditions it claims to honor into a single architectural register the synthesizer controls — is the strongest analytical challenge the project faces. The fifth-pass framing's analytical move is to replace the renovation framing the project carried in earlier passes with the cultivation framing, and to specify the analytical case for the cultivation framing's substantive answer to the decolonial critique.

### The Decolonial Critique on Its Strongest Terms

The decolonial critique runs through Mignolo's substantive engagement with the substantive operations of coloniality of being, knowledge, and power in *The Darker Side of Western Modernity* (2011) and *On Decoloniality* (2018, with Walsh); through Quijano's foundational substantive engagement with the coloniality of power; through Lugones's substantive engagement with the coloniality of gender; through Santos's substantive engagement with epistemologies of the South in *Epistemologies of the South* (2014); through Tuck and Yang's substantive analytical claim that decolonization is not metaphor in their 2012 essay; through Alcoff's substantive engagement with whiteness and decolonial commitments in *The Future of Whiteness* (2015); through the broader substantive decolonial-theory corpus.

The substantive decolonial critique of multi-traditional integrative engagement, on its strongest terms, runs as follows. Integrative engagements with multi-traditional inheritance, however well-intentioned, operate within the substantive analytical apparatus of Western modernity — its analytical categories, its philosophical frameworks, its political-economic conditions, its epistemic-institutional infrastructure. The substantive integration of underrepresented traditions into such projects, even when conducted with substantive respect for the underrepresented traditions, risks reproducing the substantive operations of coloniality by translating the underrepresented traditions into the Western analytical apparatus the project operates within; the underrepresented traditions are thereby substantively reduced to data points, sources, or resources for the Western project rather than maintained as substantive autonomous traditions on their own analytical ground. The substantive decolonial commitment requires substantive engagement with the operations of coloniality at the constitutive level — not incremental reform within colonial frameworks but substantive engagement with the structural-political-epistemic conditions under which the colonial frameworks operate.

The critique is substantive and the project takes it substantively. Any analytical engagement that claims to honor the multi-traditional canonical inheritance must engage the critique on its strongest terms and provide substantive answer rather than dismissing the critique or treating it as a problem solved by good intentions.

### Renovation as Design-Imposition; Cultivation as Substantive Alternative

The substantive analytical move of the fifth-pass framing is to specify the difference between renovation framing and cultivation framing. The renovation framing — the framing that holds that the constitutional architecture is a designed institutional fixture that can be revised through engineered amendment to produce a more adequate institutional architecture — operates within Anglo-American design-imposition assumptions that the multi-traditional foundational inheritance does not share. Under renovation framing, the analytical move from Western frame to multi-traditional canonical commitment risks operating as substantive synthesis-into-architecture: the underrepresented traditions are read as offering substantive resources for the renovated Western architecture, with the substantive risk that the integration reproduces precisely the synthesis-as-appropriation operation the decolonial critique substantively identifies.

The cultivation framing operates within different analytical assumptions. Under cultivation framing, the polity is cultivated, not designed; the conditions under which the polity grows are specified through the multi-traditional foundational inheritance the polity engages; the polity's growth into adequate honor of its inheritance is intergenerational cultivation work rather than one-shot architectural revision; the cultivation conditions are themselves diverse across the multi-traditional inheritance and are not to be synthesized into a single architectural specification. The substantive analytical commitment is that cultivation, unlike renovation, does not synthesize-into-architecture: cultivation maintains the conditions under which the traditions continue to grow alongside one another in their distinctive registers; cultivation does not produce a single architectural specification that the traditions must conform to.

### The Cultivation Framing's Substantive Answer

The substantive answer to the decolonial critique under the cultivation framing operates at four registers. First, the cultivation framing's analytical content is supplied by the Indigenous and Eastern primary articulators' own specifications of cultivation — by the Daoist *wu wei*, the Buddhist dependent origination, the Indigenous land-and-people-as-constitutive-relation, the Confucian cultivation-of-virtue-across-nested-scales, the Andean *sumak kawsay*, the Pacific Islander *Vā*, the African Ubuntu, the Maori *Whakapapa*, the seventh-generation criterion — not by Western recovery of these articulations. The Western complex-adaptive-systems, ecology, biomimicry, and disturbance-ecology canons are recent and partial recovery; the Indigenous and Eastern traditions are primary articulators of cultivation framing itself. The substantive analytical authority of the cultivation framing is supplied by the primary articulators, not by Western synthesis of them.

Second, the cultivation framing's methodological operation maintains the integrity of each tradition's primary articulations in its distinctive register. The substantive analytical commitment is that the traditions are not translated into a single architectural register but are engaged at their own analytical depth, with their own primary articulators, in their own scholarly traditions. The substantive operational test of the commitment is whether the project's downstream work maintains the integrity of the primary articulators across the project's analytical arc; the test is operational rather than declarative, and the project's fidelity to the commitment is assessed across the substantive analytical work the project produces.

Third, the cultivation framing's temporal commitment is intergenerational rather than synthesis-completing. The polity is cultivated across generations through substantive engagement with the multi-traditional foundational inheritance; the cultivation work is not completed by any one analytical pass but continues across the temporal horizon the cultivation operates over. The substantive analytical commitment is that the multi-traditional canon's substantive engagement with the cultivation work continues across generations through the substantive operational commitments the project specifies in Documents H, D-Synthesis, and G.

Fourth, the decolonial critique is preserved as live methodological discipline throughout the project's downstream work. The cultivation framing does not foreclose the decolonial critique; it specifies the analytical terrain on which the critique continues to operate. The test of the commitment is whether the project's downstream work substantively maintains the conditions under which traditions continue to grow alongside one another in their distinctive registers — and the decolonial discipline is constitutive of the substantive engagement with that test.

### The Decolonial Critique's Strongest Counterposition Engaged Analytically

The substantive decolonial counterposition the cultivation framing must address — that even cultivation framing operates from within a Western analytical apparatus and risks absorbing the traditions it claims to honor — is engaged on its strongest terms. The cultivation framing's analytical answer is that the analytical content of cultivation framing is supplied by the Indigenous and Eastern primary articulators' own specifications of cultivation; the Western analytical apparatus through which the project operates is the analytical apparatus that the project's substantive engagement with the multi-traditional inheritance is conducted within, but the substantive content is not generated from within the Western apparatus alone. The substantive philosophical authority of the cultivation framing is rooted in the primary articulators' own specifications; the Western recovery canon is read analytically as recovery rather than as foundation.
