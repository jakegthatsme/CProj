# The Neurodivergent Polis as Cultivation Condition

## Opening — The Document Under the Fifth-Pass Architecture

This document is the Part XII structural-commitment document on the neurodivergent polis as cultivation condition at the organism-and-organ-system scale. The project rests on a load-bearing triad: biocultural governance as constitutional ontology, eco-mimetic policy-making as operational method, disturbance-adapted implementation as temporal orientation. The Indigenous and Eastern traditions are primary articulators; the Western complex-adaptive-systems, ecology, biomimicry, and disturbance-ecology canons are recent and partial recovery.

Under the fifth-pass framing, the document is re-specified at three load-bearing points. First, the cognitive-plurality commitment is re-framed as substantive cultivation condition at the organism-and-organ-system scale of the nested-scales reference frame. Second, the document integrates the disability-justice and Mad-studies traditions as substantive primary articulators of cognitive-plurality cultivation conditions. Third, the document integrates the Eastern and Indigenous neurodivergent-cognate traditions at primary-articulator standing.

## Part Five — Eastern and Indigenous Neurodivergent-Cognate Traditions

The Eastern and Indigenous neurodivergent-cognate traditions stand at primary-articulator standing. The substantive engagement runs through the Buddhist *bhavanga* and Abhidhamma consciousness theory through Bhikkhu Bodhi's *A Comprehensive Manual of Abhidhamma* (1993) and the *Visuddhimagga* tradition; through Nagarjuna's Madhyamaka two-truths cognitive analysis through Jay L. Garfield's *Engaging Buddhism: Why It Matters to Philosophy* (2015); through Asanga and Vasubandhu's Yogacara consciousness-only analysis. The Daoist contemplative traditions are engaged through the *Zhuangzi*'s "Inner Chapters" on the *zhenren* (true person) and contemplative practice (Brook Ziporyn's translation) and through substantive *neidan* internal-alchemy contemplative literature. The Andean *kawsay* tradition on substantive living-cognition is engaged through PRATEC corpus and Josef Estermann's *Filosofía Andina* (1998). The Pacific Islander relational-cognition traditions are engaged through Albert Wendt's "Tatauing the Post-Colonial Body" (1996) and the substantive *Vā* relational ontology; through Upolu Lumā Vaai and Aisake Casimira's *Relational Hermeneutics: Decolonising the Mindset and the Pacific Itulagi* (2017).

The substantive analytical commitment is that these substantive traditions stand at primary-articulator standing as substantive primary articulators of substantive cognitive-plurality cultivation conditions; the substantive contemporary neurodivergent and Mad-studies traditions are read in substantive productive engagement with the Eastern and Indigenous primary articulators under the Document I (Part VII) multi-eyed-seeing methodological discipline.
