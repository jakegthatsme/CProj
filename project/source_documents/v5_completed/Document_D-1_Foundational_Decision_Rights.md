# Foundational Decision Rights

## Opening — The Document Under the Fifth-Pass Architecture

This document is the Part XII structural-commitments document specifying foundational decision rights at biosphere-and-ecozone scales. The project rests on a load-bearing triad: biocultural governance as constitutional ontology, eco-mimetic policy-making as operational method, disturbance-adapted implementation as temporal orientation. The Indigenous and Eastern traditions are primary articulators; the Western complex-adaptive-systems, ecology, biomimicry, and disturbance-ecology canons are recent and partial recovery.

Document D-1 specifies three foundational decision rights at biosphere-and-ecozone scales as bounded decision rights under the four-element specification format the entire Part XII D-series uses: scope (what the right operates over), scale (at which scale of the nested-scales reference frame), feedback condition (the eco-mimetic-sense feedback architecture Document R Part XII operationalizes), and sunset-or-adaptation condition (the temporal architecture under which the right is revised). The three rights are the unidirectional dual currency convertibility (coordinated with Document E currency architecture); the reversible amendment process; and the judicial review of executive orders.

## Part One — Unidirectional Dual Currency Convertibility as Foundational Decision Right

**Scope:** The right operates over the convertibility relation between the political-economic currency and the ecological currency, constraining the direction of conversion. The substantive specification engages the dual-currency architecture Document E (Part XII — Currency Architecture as Bounded Decision Rights and Cultivation Conditions) articulates at substantive depth; the present document specifies the substantive formal bounded-decision-right architecture.

**Scale:** Biosphere-and-ecozone scale, where the right limits political-economic decisions' capacity to absorb biospheric-scale impacts. The substantive scale-matching specification engages the nested-scales reference frame Document K (Part V) articulates in operational detail.

**Feedback condition:** The dual-currency mechanism is itself feedback architecture (cross-referenced to Document E); ongoing ecological-monitoring (cross-referenced to Document R Part XII eco-mimetic policy-making method on ecological-monitoring-style constitutional-and-statutory review) supplies the substantive empirical-feedback input. The substantive feedback architecture operates substantively across the substantive biosphere-and-ecozone scale through which the substantive currency architecture operates.

**Sunset-or-adaptation condition:** The convertibility-relation specification is subject to substantive scheduled review under specified adaptive-cycle phase (cross-referenced to Document K Thread B on panarchy substrate and Document 9 on substantive panarchy framing of transformative legislation).

## Part Two — Reversible Amendment Process as Foundational Decision Right

**Scope:** The right operates over the meta-decision of how the constitutional architecture is itself revised. Reversibility specifies substantively that no amendment is final-engineered-fixture; every amendment carries substantive provision for revision under substantive specified conditions.

**Scale:** Biosphere-and-ecozone, where the substantive meta-decision affects the substantive architecture's capacity to register and respond to biospheric-scale disturbances.

**Feedback condition:** Substantive amendment-and-reversal feedback operates through substantive specified review architecture coordinated with Document F (Part XII e-democracy as structural public check); the substantive deliberative-and-feedback architecture engages substantive cross-scale public-check feedback.

**Sunset-or-adaptation condition:** Each amendment carries substantive explicit sunset or scheduled-review specification. The substantive analytical commitment is that the substantive constitutional architecture is cultivated rather than designed-as-final-fixture; the substantive reversibility commitment is constitutive substrate of the cultivation framing's substantive analytical commitment.

The substantive engagement with the comparative-constitutional-amendment literature runs through Richard Albert's *Constitutional Amendments: Making, Breaking, and Changing Constitutions* (2019) and Sanford Levinson's *Our Undemocratic Constitution: Where the Constitution Goes Wrong (and How We the People Can Correct It)* (2006).

## Part Three — Judicial Review of Executive Orders as Foundational Decision Right

**Scope:** The right operates over executive-order outputs as substantive decisions reviewable against the substantive foundational normative commitments Document A (Part I) specifies.

**Scale:** Biosphere-and-ecozone, where substantive executive decisions have substantive aggregate-impact reach beyond decision-right scope.

**Feedback condition:** Substantive judicial review is read as substantive feedback architecture in the eco-mimetic sense (cross-referenced to Document R), not as substantive terminal-adjudication architecture. The substantive review operates substantively as substantive ongoing-sensing rather than as substantive periodic-terminal-decision.

**Sunset-or-adaptation condition:** Substantive judicial-review outcomes are themselves subject to substantive scheduled review and to the substantive reversible-amendment process Part Two specifies.

The substantive engagement runs through Mark V. Tushnet's *Weak Courts, Strong Rights: Judicial Review and Social Welfare Rights in Comparative Constitutional Law* (2008) on substantive comparative-constitutional-judicial-review engagement; through Karl E. Klare's "Legal Culture and Transformative Constitutionalism" (*South African Journal on Human Rights*, 1998); through Upendra Baxi's *The Future of Human Rights* (2002) on substantive Indian transformative-constitutionalism. The substantive coordination with Document A (Part I) operates as substantive foundational-normative substrate against which substantive executive decisions are reviewable.
