# The Physical-Environmental-Disturbance Diagnostic: Climate, Water, Fire, Geology, Contamination

## Opening — The Document Under the Fifth-Pass Architecture

This document is the Part II physical-environmental-disturbance regime diagnostic. The project rests on a load-bearing triad: biocultural governance as constitutional ontology, eco-mimetic policy-making as operational method, disturbance-adapted implementation as temporal orientation. The Indigenous and Eastern traditions are primary articulators; the Western complex-adaptive-systems, ecology, biomimicry, and disturbance-ecology canons are recent and partial recovery.

The physical-environmental-disturbance regime threatens the physical-environmental substrate of the biocultural polity through climate, hydrological regime shift, fire regime shift, geological events, anthropogenic chemical contamination, and atmospheric composition shift. The document operationalizes the substantive analytical engagement with each dimension; the climate-justice canon and the Indigenous water-and-fire-sovereignty primary articulators stand at primary-articulator standing. Coordination with Document 8 (Part IX — climate as paradigmatic stress test of the constitutional architecture) is constitutive; Document T provides the substantive regime diagnostic, Document 8 carries the substantive constitutional stress-test reading.
