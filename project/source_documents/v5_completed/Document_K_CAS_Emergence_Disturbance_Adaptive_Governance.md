## Part Four — The Nested-Scales Reference Frame in Operational Detail

The nested-scales reference frame is articulated in operational detail as constitutional category. The frame specifies seven spatial scales — biosphere, ecozone, ecosystem, habitat, population, organism, organ system — and their temporal analogs, with substantive engagement with the characteristic spatial extent, temporal extent, kinds of decision rights appropriately matched, and scale-mismatch failure modes at each scale.

Scale-mismatch failure modes at biosphere scale include single-jurisdiction decisions absorbing planetary-scale impacts (climate emissions at single-state scale producing biosphere-scale outcomes), short-temporal-horizon decisions producing biosphere-scale legacy impacts, financial-market-time-scale decisions producing biosphere-scale outcomes across geologically-significant timescales.
