# Intergenerational Rectification as Cultivation Work

## Opening — The Document Under the Fifth-Pass Architecture

This document is the Part XII structural-commitment document on intergenerational rectification as cultivation work across temporal scales. The project rests on a load-bearing triad: biocultural governance as constitutional ontology, eco-mimetic policy-making as operational method, disturbance-adapted implementation as temporal orientation. The Indigenous and Eastern traditions are primary articulators; the Western complex-adaptive-systems, ecology, biomimicry, and disturbance-ecology canons are recent and partial recovery.

Under the fifth-pass framing, the document is re-specified at four load-bearing points. First, the terminological shift from "multi-generational" (existing project record) to "intergenerational" (fifth-pass terminology) is propagated throughout. Second, the rectification commitment is re-framed as substantive scale-matched cultivation work across temporal scales: the temporal-analog scales of the nested-scales reference frame (deep time, civilizational time, multi-generational time, generational time, inter-decadal time, biographical time, organism-internal time) specify where rectification work operates at which temporal scale. Third, the Haudenosaunee seventh-generation criterion is integrated as primary articulation of the intergenerational cultivation commitment. Fourth, the document integrates Edith Brown Weiss's intergenerational equity, Stephen Gardiner's climate-and-time ethics, the Long Now Foundation corpus, and Rob Nixon's slow-violence framework alongside Andean *Pachakuti*, Buddhist *anicca*, Daoist temporal flow, Hopi temporal cosmology, and Aboriginal Dreamtime as substantive primary-articulator temporal traditions.
