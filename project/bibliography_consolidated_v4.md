# Consolidated Bibliography — Full Project Arc, v4
## Organized by Analytical Theme, Co-Equally Integrating Western, Indigenous, Eastern, Black-Radical, Dalit, Disability-Justice, Neurodivergent, Feminist, Latinx, Asian-American, Decolonial, Climate-Justice, Islamic-Political-Philosophical, and Liberation-Theology Traditions

*This bibliography is organized first by analytical theme. Each thematic section integrates the named scholarly traditions as co-equal contributors. The fourth-pass revision extends the third-pass architecture by giving Eastern philosophical traditions (Daoist, Confucian, Madhyamaka and Yogacara Buddhist, classical Indian polity, Islamic political philosophy, Neo-Confucian, Kyoto School) co-equal standing with the Indigenous and Western traditions, and by adding eleven new thematic Parts: Complex Adaptive Systems and Emergence (Part XIV); Multilevel Selection and Evolution of Cooperation (Part XV); Sub-Species Evolution and the Analytical Status of the Human (Part XVI); Adaptive Leadership and Bounded-Cognition-at-Scale (Part XVII); Eastern Philosophical Traditions (Part XVIII, with sub-sections for Daoist, Confucian, Buddhist, Hindu/Classical-Indian, Islamic, Neo-Confucian, and Kyoto School); Comparative Political Economy as Multi-Traditional Genealogy (Part XIX); Comparative Anthropology of Governance Across Human History (Part XX); Modern Empirical Examples of CAS-Informed Governance (Part XXI); Time, Long-Term Thinking, and Intergenerational Justice (Part XXII); Systems Thinking and Cybernetics (Part XXIII); Multi-Lens Checks and Balances (Part XXIV).*

*Indigenous scholars are cited with their tribal or national affiliation in parentheses on first reference. Where a primary text is engaged on its own constitutional or analytical authority, the document itself is treated as primary; the Indigenous-authored scholarly engagement is treated as primary scholarly authority alongside it; the non-Indigenous scholarly engagement is treated as secondary commentary. The same convention is extended to Eastern and other non-Western traditions: where a primary text is engaged on its own analytical authority (the Daodejing, the Analects, the Mūlamadhyamakakārikā, the Arthashastra, the Qur'an, the Vinaya), the document itself is primary; tradition-internal scholarly authority is primary scholarly authority alongside it; outside-tradition scholarly engagement is secondary commentary.*

*Document codes: 1–10 = the existing numeric documents; A–L = the lettered documents under the Roman-numeraled architecture (A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, J, K, L). Cross-references between thematic Parts of this bibliography are noted where an entry is load-bearing in multiple themes; entries are not duplicated but cross-referenced.*
