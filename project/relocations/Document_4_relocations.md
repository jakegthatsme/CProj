# Document 4 → Document L Relocations

Holding file specifying which sections of the v4 Document 4 source
(`project/source_documents/Founders_Biology_Ecology_Anthropology.md`) relocate
to Document L (Part IV — Deep Substrate) in the fifth pass, which sections
stay in Document 4, and what Document L must compose fresh that the Document 4
substrate does not supply.

The move-don't-regenerate principle governs. Relocated material is preserved
in its move (not duplicated, not rewritten); Document 4 retains the founding-
era critique focused on the four design-imposition framings (Plan 4 Threads B
through F); Document L absorbs the relocated material as cultivation substrate
under the Part IV scope (multilevel selection, hunter-gatherer cognitive
baseline, cultural-evolutionary scaling mechanisms, comparative anthropology
of governance, sub-species evolutionary backdrop).

---

## Sections that relocate from Document 4 to Document L

**Part Two §II — "The Actual Scale of Human Political Organization."**
Sub-sections "Dunbar's Number and the Constitutional Scale Problem" and
"Kinship, Reciprocity, and the Foundations of Political Order" relocate to
Document L's hunter-gatherer cognitive-baseline section and to the
cultural-evolutionary-scaling-mechanisms section. Under Document L's framing
this is read not as critique of the founding-era assumption but as substantive
specification of the organism-and-organ-system scale of the nested-scales
reference frame and of the cultural-evolutionary scaling mechanisms (norms,
ritual, prestige, fictive kinship, imagined communities) through which
small-band cognition is extended to large-scale political units.

**Part Four §III — "Group Selection and the Paradox of Civic Virtue."**
The "Evolution of Cooperation" treatment relocates to Document L's
multilevel-selection section. Under Document L's framing this is the Sober-
and-Wilson, Boyd-and-Richerson, Henrich substrate read as cultivation-
substrate specification rather than as founding-era critique.

**Part Four §IV — "Behavioral Ecology, Agriculture, and the Jeffersonian
Myth."** The "Hunter-Gatherer Legacy" treatment relocates to Document L's
hunter-gatherer cognitive-baseline section and to the comparative-anthropology
archive. Under Document L's framing the Jeffersonian-myth critique stays in
Document 4 (the Jefferson-as-cultivator strand is engaged in Document 3 and
in Document 4's design-imposition critique); the hunter-gatherer-cognitive-
legacy substantive content relocates as cultivation-substrate specification.

## What Document L composes fresh (not in Document 4 substrate)

The sub-species evolutionary backdrop (Pääbo's *Neanderthal Man* of 2014;
Higham's *The World Before Us* of 2021; Wragg Sykes's *Kindred* of 2020) as
evidence that the human is an analytically contestable category emerging
from multi-species coexistence and absorption — Document 4 does not
substantively engage this material; Document L composes it fresh.

The cultural-evolutionary scaling mechanisms (norms, ritual, prestige,
fictive kinship, imagined communities) under Boyd-and-Richerson's *Culture
and the Evolutionary Process* of 1985 and *Not by Genes Alone* of 2005,
Henrich's *The Secret of Our Success* of 2015 and *The WEIRDest People in
the World* of 2020 — Document 4 contains the hunter-gatherer-cognitive-
legacy substrate that relocates, but the cultural-evolutionary scaling
mechanisms as analytical apparatus must be composed fresh in Document L.

The comparative-anthropology-of-governance archive across hunter-gatherer,
horticultural, agrarian, pastoral, industrial, post-industrial, and
contemporary digital-network political units — Document 4 contains the
hunter-gatherer slice; Document L composes the cross-formation archive
fresh, drawing on Graeber and Wengrow's *The Dawn of Everything* of 2021,
James C. Scott's *Against the Grain* of 2017 and *The Art of Not Being
Governed* of 2009, Marshall Sahlins, Pierre Clastres, and the comparative
political-anthropology literature.

The nation-state literature as theoretical archive (Gellner, Anderson,
Hobsbawm, Anthony Smith, Tilly, James C. Scott) — Document 4 does not
substantively engage; Document L composes fresh.

The Eastern and Indigenous primary-articulator engagement on comparative
governance formations across the nested-scales reference frame — Document 4
does not engage; Document L composes fresh under the Document I (Part VII)
multi-eyed-seeing methodological discipline.
