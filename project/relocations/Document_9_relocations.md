# Document 9 → Document L Relocations

Holding file specifying the coordination between the v4 Document 9 sources
(`project/source_documents/doc9_transformative_legislation.md` and
`project/source_documents/Document_Nine_Political_Economy_Transformative_Legislation.md`;
both listed as Document 9 source files in the fifth-pass planning document)
and Document L (Part IV — Deep Substrate) on the comparative-political-
economy material that the planning document specifies as relocating to
Document L's comparative-anthropology-of-governance archive.

The Document 9 → Document L relocation is the smallest of the three holding-
file coordinations. Document 9's primary substrate is American legislative-
wave political-economy historical archive (Progressive Era, New Deal, Civil
Rights, Environmental Wave, Great Society, ACA, IRA), which under the fifth-
pass framing is read substantively as panarchy-cycle archive within Document
9 itself. The relocation to Document L is therefore not section-extraction
but **cross-citation specification**: Document L cites Document 9 for the
post-industrial American slice of the comparative-anthropology-of-governance
archive, and Document 9 retains its full historical-wave treatment under
the panarchy-cycle re-framing.

---

## Document 9's analytical register

Per the fifth-pass planning document, Document 9's re-specification re-frames
the historical-and-contemporary diagnostic under panarchy-cycle framing as
substantive (rather than as metaphor); integrates multi-traditional
comparative-political-economy material; integrates the material-substrate
documents (Documents N, O, P, Q) and Document R on eco-mimetic policy-making.
The historical-wave archive (Part One of both source files) is the
substantive substrate Document 9 carries at the political-economy register.

## Document L's analytical register

Document L's comparative-anthropology-of-governance archive spans hunter-
gatherer, horticultural, agrarian, pastoral, industrial, post-industrial,
and contemporary digital-network political units as the empirical archive
of cultivation conditions and their variations. Document 9's substrate
contributes selectively to the post-industrial and contemporary slices of
this archive.
