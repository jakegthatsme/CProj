# Document 7 → Document L Relocations

Holding file specifying the coordination between the v4 Document 7 source
(`project/source_documents/doc7_minds_markets_sacred.md`) and Document L
(Part IV — Deep Substrate) on the cultural-evolutionary-mechanisms material
that the planning document specifies as relocating from Document 7 to
Document L.

The relocation here is more delicate than the Document 4 → Document L
relocation. Document 7 engages substantial cultural-evolutionary and
comparative-anthropology material at §2.7 (The Tertiary Lens — Anthropology
and Behavior: The Fiction of Homo Economicus) and §§3.4–3.6 (Indigenous
Knowledge as Living Governance; Haudenosaunee Great Law; Buen Vivir, Ubuntu),
and this material is integral to Document 7's macroeconomic critique and
sacred-values critique. The relocation is therefore not section-extraction
but **shared-substrate specification**: the same primary-source archive is
engaged by both documents at their distinct analytical registers, and the
relocation specifies what analytical work each document carries and where
the duplication is avoided through cross-citation.

---

## Document 7's analytical register on this substrate

Document 7 engages the cultural-evolutionary substrate at two registers:
the **anthropology-of-economic-behavior critique of Homo Economicus** (§2.7)
and the **incommensurability-of-values critique of universal fungibility**
(§§3.1–3.7). Under the fifth-pass framing per the planning document, Document
7's substantive task re-frames the decision-science material as specification
of the organism-and-organ-system scale of the nested-scales reference frame;
the macroeconomic critique as specification of the ecosystem-and-ecozone
scale; and the sacred-values material as specification of cultivation
conditions under which decisions at different scales can be coordinated
without collapsing into commensurability. The cultural-evolutionary substrate
serves these analytical purposes in Document 7 and is preserved at the
analytical register Document 7 specifies.

## Document L's analytical register on this substrate

Document L engages the cultural-evolutionary substrate at the **cultivation-
substrate** register: hunter-gatherer cognitive baseline as the organism-
and-organ-system scale of the nested-scales reference frame, cultural-
evolutionary scaling mechanisms (norms, ritual, prestige, fictive kinship,
imagined communities) as cultivation conditions under which larger-scale
polities have grown, comparative-anthropology-of-governance archive as
empirical archive of cultivation conditions and their variations across
hunter-gatherer, horticultural, agrarian, pastoral, industrial, post-
industrial, and contemporary digital-network political units.

## Shared primary sources and their dual analytical engagement

**David Graeber and David Wengrow, *The Dawn of Everything* (2021).**
Document 7 §2.7 engages this work for the critique of social-evolutionary
stage-narrative and the inversion of the standard hunter-gatherer-to-
agriculture-to-hierarchy progression underlying Homo Economicus. Document L
engages the same work as foundational empirical archive for the comparative-
anthropology-of-governance scope. The work is cited in both documents at
their respective analytical registers; the substantive primary-source
engagement is composed in Document L (which carries the comparative-
anthropology-of-governance archive at Part IV depth) and referenced in
Document 7 (which uses the work to make the economic-anthropology point).

**Marshall Sahlins, *Stone Age Economics* of 1972 (including "The Original
Affluent Society").** Same dual engagement: Document 7 for the inversion of
the hunter-gatherer-poverty narrative underlying market-economic anthropology;
Document L for the substantive comparative-anthropology-of-governance
treatment at hunter-gatherer scale.

**Christopher Boehm, *Hierarchy in the Forest* (1999).** Substantively
relocates to Document L's hunter-gatherer cognitive-baseline section (shared
with the Document 4 relocation specification for Part Four §III); Document 7
cites Document L for the substantive treatment and retains brief reference at
the analytical-economic-behavior register.

**Joseph Henrich, *The WEIRDest People in the World* (2020) and *The Secret
of Our Success* (2015).** Substantively relocates to Document L's cultural-
evolutionary scaling-mechanisms section; Document 7 cites Document L for the
substantive treatment and retains brief reference at the WEIRD-psychology
critique of Homo Economicus.

**Marcel Mauss, *The Gift* (1925); Alan Fiske, *Structures of Social Life*
(1991); Bowles and Gintis, *A Cooperative Species* (2011); Ernst Fehr and
Simon Gächter, "Altruistic Punishment in Humans" (*Nature*, 2002).** These
remain primarily in Document 7 as the substantive substrate of the §2.7
anthropology-of-economic-behavior critique. Document L cites Document 7 for
the substantive treatment and integrates the gift-economy and strong-
reciprocity material into the cultural-evolutionary-scaling-mechanisms
section at the cultivation-substrate register.

**Michael Tomasello, *A Natural History of Human Morality* (2016) and
related shared-intentionality work.** Substantively relocates to Document L
(shared with the Document 4 Plan 4 Thread B substrate on cooperative-
cognitive-primate evidence); Document 7 cites Document L for the substantive
treatment.

**Elinor Ostrom, *Governing the Commons* (1990) and the commons-management
corpus.** Remains primarily in Document 7 (and substantively engaged in
Document Q on cradle-to-cradle and Document P on food-soil-agriculture);
Document L cites Ostrom in the cultural-evolutionary-scaling-mechanisms
treatment of polycentric commons institutions as cultivation conditions at
habitat-and-population scales.

## Sections of Document 7 that relocate substantively

**§2.7 sub-sections on Graeber-Wengrow, Sahlins, Boehm, Henrich, and
Tomasello** — relocate to Document L as substantive primary-source treatment
at the cultivation-substrate register. The §2.7 analytical framing on Homo
Economicus is preserved in Document 7 with reference to Document L for the
substantive treatment of these primary sources.

**§§3.4–3.6 (Indigenous Knowledge as Living Governance; Haudenosaunee Great
Law; Buen Vivir, Ubuntu)** — partially relocate to Document L's comparative-
anthropology-of-governance archive. The sacred-values and incommensurability
analytical register stays in Document 7. The substantive primary-source
treatment of the Haudenosaunee Great Law, Andean Buen Vivir, and African
Ubuntu as governance formations at scale relocates to Document L's
comparative-anthropology archive; Document 7 cites Document L for the
substantive treatment and retains the sacred-values analytical framing.

## What Document L composes fresh (not in Document 7 substrate)

The cross-formation comparative-anthropology-of-governance archive at
horticultural, agrarian, pastoral, industrial, post-industrial, and
contemporary digital-network scales — Document 7's substrate covers hunter-
gatherer and Indigenous-governance formations; Document L composes the
intermediate-and-contemporary formations fresh, drawing on James C. Scott,
Pierre Clastres, and the comparative political-anthropology literature.

The cultural-evolutionary scaling mechanisms as analytical apparatus
(norms, ritual, prestige, fictive kinship, imagined communities and their
characteristic failure modes as small-band cognition is extended to large-
scale political units) — Document 7's substrate engages primary sources
(Henrich) but does not articulate the scaling-mechanisms apparatus as
analytical category; Document L composes the apparatus fresh.

The nation-state literature as theoretical archive (Gellner, Anderson,
Hobsbawm, Anthony Smith, Tilly, James C. Scott) — not in Document 7
substrate; Document L composes fresh.

## Operational notes

The relocation is shared-substrate specification rather than section
extraction. The Document 7 drafting session reads this file to understand
where substantive primary-source treatment is being moved to Document L and
where Document 7 retains analytical-framing engagement with reference to
Document L. The Document L drafting session reads this file plus the
Document 4 and Document 9 relocations files to assemble the relocated
material under the Part IV framing. Cross-citation between Document 7 and
Document L resolves the duplication question: each document cites the other
for the substantive treatment at the other's analytical register.
