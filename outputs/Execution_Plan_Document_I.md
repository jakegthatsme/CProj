The Indigenous and Eastern traditions are the principle's primary articulators; the Western complex-adaptive-systems, ecology, biomimicry, and disturbance-ecology canons are recent and partial recovery. "Grown, not built" is usable as colloquial summary phrase.

For Document I, the triad bears on what multi-eyed seeing is methodologically obligated to deliver: under the fifth-pass framing, multi-eyed seeing is the methodological discipline by which the biocultural-governance ontology's commitment to multi-traditional canonical co-equality is operationalized in analytical and citation practice, with the Eastern epistemic traditions integrated at primary-articulator standing alongside the Indigenous-articulator standing the existing source establishes through the Marshall family's Two-Eyed Seeing.
